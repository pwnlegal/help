// WHAT THIS IS

If you have a legal question about security research, but cannot 
access specialist legal advice, I will try to help you for free.

======================================================================
  
// WHAT I HELP WITH

- Computer misuse law — “is what I’m doing illegal?”
- Responsible disclosure — how to claim vulns without getting sued
- Vendor threats — cease and desist, handling legal intimidation
- Bug bounty — terms, scope, safe harbour
- Employment — questionable instructions, bad practices
- Police — what to do if you are approached
- Anything where hacking and the law collide

======================================================================

// WHOAMI

I’m Richard Hanstock, a barrister specialising in cyber security law.
I live in Gloucestershire and practice from chambers in London, UK.

=====================================================================
  
// HOW IT WORKS

Message me, describe your situation. I’ll give you a straight answer, 
usually within a few days.

I’ll ask enough to check for conflicts, but I don’t need your real
name for an initial conversation.

If your problem is simple, that’s the end of it. If it needs sustained
work, I’ll explain how a formal engagement works.

======================================================================
  
// WHY ARE YOU DOING THIS?

Security research is hugely important, yet the law leaves researchers 
exposed to legal risk, even when they're acting in good faith. I want 
to do my part to help to close that gap.

What's more, we all make mistakes. Everyone deserves access to legal 
advice, even if they've done something wrong.
